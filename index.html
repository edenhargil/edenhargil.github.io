About

Eden Har‑Gil is a New York-based drummer and composer. Originally from Kibbutz Yakum, Israel, Eden fell in love with music in his early childhood while learning piano and drums. While in high school, he studied with David Rich for 7 years. From 2015 to 2017, he studied at The Center for Jazz Studies at the Israeli Conservatory of Music in Tel Aviv. While studying at the conservatory, Eden had the opportunity to study with some of the best musicians in Israel including Mark Moshayev, Anat Fort, Omri Mor, Avi Lebovich, Roni Iwrin, Danny Rosenfeld, and more. During his studies, Eden established himself as an active drummer in the Israeli jazz scene.
In 2018, Eden relocated to the U.S to complete his academic studies at William Paterson University and further his musical career. As recipient of both the Presidential and the Rory D. Quince Endowed Scholarships, Eden was privileged to study with jazz greats such as Horacee Arnold, Bill Charlap, Jeremy Pelt, Cecil Bridgewater, Pete McGuinness and Kevin Norton. He Graduated Magna cum Laude in the spring of 2020. Since 2018, while also gigging as a sideman, Eden has been leading his own quartet which is planned to record its debut album soon.
Alongside his career in music performance, Eden is also a passionate educator.
He has been teaching drums privately since 2014 and has experience working with all levels and ages. In addition to drum instruction, Eden is also giving lessons on music theory and composition.
Students who might be interested in taking lessons are encouraged to contact Eden through this website.
