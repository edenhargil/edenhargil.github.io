About the Album

Hearts of Palm is the culmination of my path and development as a jazz performer and composer. Over a decade has passed since I’ve made the decision to devote myself to music and the craft of music creation. That decision has led me across oceans, countries, and institutions and exposed me to new encounters with people, cultures, and ideas. The compositions presented on this album all draw from old and recent experiences that I’ve had. It was important for me to create this album in the most honest way I could within the limitations of this project. Most of the tunes were composed especially for this project (all except “Leaf” which was previously recorded with a piano trio). I felt recording new music would lead to a more genuine message even if it meant the development of the tunes had to be done in a expedited manner. The album comprises six original compositions that explore the diversity of textures and moods available to the band’s unusual orchestration and the exciting choice of personnel in this session.
I am joined on the album by an exciting talented group of musicians, including Dezron Douglas on bass, Caroline Davis on alto saxophone, and Lo Wood on baritone saxophone.

About the Artist

Originally from Kibbutz Yakum, Israel, Eden Har‑Gil is a New York-based drummer and composer.
He has been honing his skills under the tutelage of many jazz legends such as Lenny White, Billy Drummond, Bill Charlap, Horacee Arnold and Anat Fort.
Since his move to New York City in 2018, Eden has played and collaborated with international musicians like
Dave Pietro, Martin Wind, Steve LaSpina, Dezron Douglas, Caroline Davis, Lo Wood, Marrtin Nevin, Hillai Govreen and others.
Creating different projects and bands in the last couple of years (among those were the exploration of the music of Thelonious Monk, Andrew Hill),
Eden is now finishing his debut album “Hearts of Palm” which will be released in 2024.
The album features original compositions that present Eden’s unique sound and his world-class partners.

Tracks

  1. Hearts of Palm

    “Hearts of Palm” was the core of the inspiration for this project. The opening phrase of the horns was the fountain from which the rest of the album emerged. It is dedicated to my ex-partner, Adi, and the breakup we went through this last year.

  2. Amarna

    “Amarna” is my homage to Wayne Shorter R.I.P, who passed away this year. I have studied the music of Shorter in the past year at NYU with Professor Dave Pietro and was deeply inspired by his writing.

  3. Embedded

    “Pinks” was composed last of all the tunes. It was written along with the cherries blossoming all over New York City and thus it got its name. I wanted this song to reflect the change of seasons, the coming of spring, the wind and what it can bring.

  4. Pinks

    “Embedded” came out of a moment I was bound to my apartment, to my bed to be exact without the will or ability to get out. Since its initial birth the tune has gone many revisions and the result as a mix of emotions that I hope reflects a complex picture of reality.

  5. Leaf

    “Leaf” is a composition that I have been playing in many different bands and projects. It holds a certain truth that keeps being relevant to every change I go through. The tune was in a way rewritten for the unique instrumentation of this album.

  6. Nethermead

    “Nethermead” is named after Prospect Park, my safe haven in the past turmoilous months. It contains the greens of grass, shade of trees, the run of dogs, the play of kids, the jog of parents and the sigh of elders.
